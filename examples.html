The Many Faces of Identity Theft

There are so many different kinds of identity theft. Here are 6 kinds:

  • Financial Identity Theft
  • Child Identity Theft
  • Criminal Identity Theft
  • Synthetic Identity Theft
  • Medical Identity Theft
  • Identity Cloning & Concealment

Financial Identity Theft

Financial identity theft is one of the most common types of identity theft. It occurs when an individual uses another person’s information for their own personal profit. An example of this would be a fraudster using your credit card number to buy plushies on Amazon. In financial identity theft, your personal information may also be used to make a new bank account under your name. Any benefits that you may receive get redirected to the fraud.

Child Identity Theft

Child identity theft is a growing issue where the identities of minors are exploited. This is because most children under 18 don’t have credit reports, their information can be used to obtain anything from licenses to even property. Many child identity theft victims only find about the fraud when they get older and try to apply for credit.

Criminal Identity Theft

Criminal identity theft occurs when someone who is apprehended provides your information to law enforcement. This causes the charges to be placed under your name and allow the criminal to get off relatively scot-free. Victims can only discover this type of fraud when consequences arise (you receive a court summons) or when they do background checks. One of the issues with criminal identity theft is that it is difficult to clear one’s name. It may even lead to job rejections if not dealt with properly (as the victim would have a criminal record until those charges are cleared).

Synthetic Identity Theft

Synthetic identity theft involves fraudsters creating fake identities using any combination of fake and real information. They then use those identities to commit more crimes and frauds. An example of this would be an identity thief using your social insurance number and your address alongside a fake name and phone number. The targets of synthetic identity theft are usually children or deceased individuals, as their information is not as tightly monitored.

Medical Identity Theft

Medical identity theft happens when someone seeks medical attention, obtains prescription drugs, or sends health insurance providers fake claims using your own personal information. Not only does this harm your financial records, but it might also disrupt your own medical care, as the identity thief’s medical records may be added to your own. This may keep you from getting the appropriate coverage or medical care. Insurance identity theft is a more broad form of medical identity theft.

Identity Cloning & Concealment

Identity cloning involves the identity thief impersonating someone else to conceal their own identity. An example of this would be illegal immigrants using fake names and other information in order to hide the fact that they are illegal. Unlike other forms of identity theft, where they are usually discovered if enough debt or crimes are racked up, identity cloning can continue perpetually without being detected. This may especially be the case if the identity thief can get forgeries of important documents to pass checks for authenticity.

In 2014, identity thieves stole $16 billion from 12.7 million identity fraud victims, according to Javelin Strategy & Research.
