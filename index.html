Identity Theft

And how it can affect you.

What is identity theft?

Picture this. You're browsing on the internet and you suddenly find that you can't access your banking account. You check your other accounts and find you can't access them either... chances are, you've become a victim of identity theft. It's important to remember that identity theft can't only happen with the loss of your wallet. Identity theft is a serious crime, and it is one where your personal information becomes jeopardized. This can happen through a variety of ways (the criminal can do anything from rummaging in your dumpster to hacking your accounts).

"If we don't act now to safeguard our privacy, we could all become victims of identity theft."

- Bill Nelson

Personally Identifiable Information includes:

  • Full Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Social Security Number
  • Fingerprints
  • Passwords
  • Mailing Address
  • PINs
  • Credit Card Numbers
  • Bank Account Numbers

Well, I don't have anything to hide. Why does it matter?


The fraudster can rack up significant debt in your name, leave you with a criminal record, and do much more with just your Social Security Number. Identity theft is commonly used for the criminal to either avoid consequences under their name (and so they use yours), or to use your connections and hard work for their own gain. Victims of identity theft might not realize they are being targeted until they check their bank statements, which, depending on the person, might be months. The effects of identity theft might last anywhere from several days to many years, depending on the severity. You definitely do not want to be a victim of identity theft, even if you think you have nothing to hide.

47% of Americans experienced financial identity theft in 2020.
